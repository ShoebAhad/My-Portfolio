Publications

DECPA-FL: Dynamic Ensemble Clustering for Poison-Aware Federated Learning under Adversarial Conditions

Ahad Bin Islam Shoeb∗, Kamrul Hasan†, Liang Hong†, Tariqul Islam‡, Imtiaz Ahmed§, Zoheb Hasan¶, Sumit Chakravarty∥ ∗Dhaka University, Dhaka, Bangladesh †Tennessee State University, Nashville, TN, USA ‡Syracuse University, Syracuse, NY, USA §Howard University, Washington, DC, USA ¶Laval University, Quebec City, QC, Canada ∥Kennesaw State University, Marietta, GA, USA

IEEE GlobeCom 2025
Download PDF

This research addresses a critical challenge in federated learning: defending against poisoning attacks while maintaining system performance. Traditional federated learning systems are vulnerable to malicious participants who can compromise the global model through data or model poisoning. Our proposed DECPA-FL framework introduces a novel dynamic ensemble clustering approach that intelligently identifies and isolates adversarial participants without compromising the collaborative learning process. The methodology combines advanced clustering techniques with ensemble learning to create a robust defense mechanism. We developed dynamic clustering algorithms that adapt to changing attack patterns and participant behaviors in real-time. The system maintains multiple model clusters and uses ensemble voting to determine the most reliable global model updates, effectively filtering out poisoned contributions. Our experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in both attack detection accuracy and model performance retention compared to existing defense mechanisms. The framework successfully maintained over 90% of clean model accuracy even under sophisticated poisoning attacks, while traditional approaches showed substantial degradation. This work represents a major step forward in securing federated learning systems for real-world deployment. The research has practical implications for healthcare, finance, and IoT applications where data privacy and model integrity are paramount. Working on this project deepened my understanding of adversarial machine learning and reinforced my commitment to developing trustworthy AI systems. The collaborative nature of this international research, spanning institutions across multiple countries, provided invaluable experience in global academic cooperation and cross-cultural research methodologies.

UNHCR Report-Bangla

This books was written by me based on the Report of UNHCR on July Uprising-2024

Students against Discrimination 2025
Download PDF

The July Uprising of 2024 marked a pivotal moment in Bangladesh's history, representing a significant student-led movement for democratic reform and social justice. Recognizing the importance of making international human rights documentation accessible to Bengali-speaking communities, I undertook the translation of the official UNHCR report documenting these historic events. This translation project was motivated by the critical need to bridge language barriers in human rights reporting. Many Bangladeshi citizens, particularly those directly affected by the uprising, could not access the original English report despite its relevance to their experiences and rights. The translation serves as a vital resource for local communities, civil society organizations, and legal advocates working on accountability and justice initiatives. The translation process required careful attention to legal terminology, cultural context, and sensitive content related to human rights violations. I worked closely with human rights experts and Bengali language scholars to ensure accuracy while maintaining the report's authoritative tone and legal precision. Special consideration was given to translating complex legal concepts and international human rights standards into accessible Bengali. Published in collaboration with Students against Discrimination, this work has been widely distributed among student organizations, civil society groups, and affected communities. The Bengali version has facilitated broader public understanding of international human rights perspectives on the uprising and strengthened advocacy efforts for accountability. This project reinforced my commitment to using my skills for social justice and human rights advocacy. The experience of translating such significant historical documentation highlighted the power of language accessibility in democratic participation. It also deepened my understanding of the intersection between technology, language, and social movements, inspiring my continued involvement in projects that serve marginalized communities and promote transparency in governance.

Purpose Statement

Why I'm Pursuing Leadership, Policy Dialogue & Diplomacy Programs

My journey as a youth leader and social entrepreneur has been fundamentally shaped by a deep belief that transformative change occurs at the intersection of grassroots activism and policy innovation. Through my experiences leading student movements, founding educational platforms, and working with government institutions, I have witnessed firsthand how effective leadership can bridge the gap between community needs and institutional solutions.

The July 2025 uprising in Bangladesh, where I served as a key organizer, demonstrated the power of strategic youth leadership in driving democratic reform. Our movement's success in abolishing discriminatory quota systems and promoting democratic governance reinforced my conviction that young leaders must be equipped with sophisticated policy analysis skills and diplomatic acumen to navigate complex political landscapes effectively.

My work founding IT For All and Academic Affiliation, which has trained over 5,000 students including 2,000+ women across Bangladesh, UK, and Australia, has taught me that sustainable social change requires not just passion, but also strategic policy frameworks and cross-cultural collaboration. These experiences have highlighted the critical importance of understanding global governance structures, international development policies, and diplomatic protocols.

Through my environmental conservation initiatives, including collaboration with the Ministry of Agriculture for the Tree Fair 2025, I have learned that addressing climate change and environmental degradation requires multilateral cooperation and sophisticated policy dialogue. These challenges transcend national boundaries and demand leaders who can navigate complex international negotiations and build consensus across diverse stakeholder groups.

My recognition with a full scholarship to the Dubai World Youth Leadership Conference validates my commitment to developing the next generation of policy leaders. However, I recognize that to maximize my impact, I need advanced training in diplomatic protocols, international relations theory, policy analysis frameworks, and cross-cultural negotiation strategies.

I am particularly drawn to programs that emphasize the intersection of youth leadership and policy innovation because I believe that the most pressing global challenges – from climate change to educational inequality to democratic governance – require fresh perspectives combined with institutional knowledge. My goal is to develop the skills necessary to translate grassroots energy into effective policy solutions and to build bridges between diverse communities and formal governance structures.

Ultimately, I envision myself as a leader who can operate effectively across multiple domains – from local community organizing to international diplomatic forums. I aspire to contribute to a more just, sustainable, and inclusive world by developing policy solutions that are both innovative and implementable, and by fostering dialogue that transcends traditional boundaries of geography, culture, and ideology.
