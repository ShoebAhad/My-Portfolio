Purpose Statement

Advancing AI Research Through Interdisciplinary PhD & MSc Studies

My pursuit of PhD and MSc programs in Cybersecurity, Agri-AI, Health-AI, Machine Learning, HCI and NLP, Data Science, and Data Engineering stems from a deep conviction that the greatest technological breakthroughs emerge at the intersection of multiple disciplines. Through my published research in IEEE CCNC 2025 on adversarial federated learning and my work as a Machine Learning Engineer at Turing on Google DeepMind projects, I have witnessed how interdisciplinary approaches can address complex global challenges in cybersecurity, healthcare, and agricultural sustainability.

In Cybersecurity and Adversarial AI: My DECPA-FL research demonstrates advanced capabilities in securing distributed AI systems against sophisticated attacks. I am passionate about advancing research in privacy-preserving machine learning, federated learning security, and adversarial robustness. The increasing deployment of AI in critical infrastructure demands innovative security solutions that I am uniquely positioned to develop through rigorous doctoral study.

In Agricultural and Health AI: My undergraduate thesis on STPS-NET for urban agriculture and my professional experience developing 95% accurate cardiovascular risk prediction models showcase my commitment to applying AI for societal benefit. I seek to advance research in precision agriculture, medical informatics, and sustainable technology solutions that address food security and healthcare accessibility challenges globally.

In Machine Learning and Data Science: My experience with reinforcement learning (RLHF, RLEF, SFT), transformer architectures, and large-scale data processing using Apache Spark and Kafka provides a solid foundation for advancing theoretical and applied ML research. I am particularly interested in developing explainable AI systems, efficient training methodologies, and scalable data engineering solutions for next-generation AI applications.

In HCI and NLP: Through founding IT For All and training over 5,000 students including 2,000+ women, I have gained deep insights into human-AI interaction and inclusive technology design. My multilingual background and international collaboration experience position me to contribute to cross-cultural AI research, multimodal interaction systems, and accessible AI interfaces.

My research philosophy emphasizes ethical AI development, international collaboration, and real-world impact. Having worked with institutions across the US, Canada, UK, and Bangladesh, I understand that the most impactful research transcends geographical and cultural boundaries. I am committed to developing AI systems that are not only technically sophisticated but also socially responsible and globally accessible.

I seek PhD and MSc programs that offer rigorous theoretical foundations while providing opportunities for interdisciplinary collaboration and real-world application. My goal is to contribute to the advancement of AI research that addresses pressing global challenges in cybersecurity, healthcare, agriculture, and human-computer interaction, while fostering inclusive and ethical technology development practices.

Ultimately, I envision myself as a research leader who bridges academic excellence with practical innovation, contributing to the global AI research community while addressing critical societal challenges through responsible and impactful technology solutions.
